## Lesson: Basic Introductions! (Ages 5-7) **1. Learning Objectives:** * Learn how to say hello and goodbye. * Learn how to introduce yourself by saying your name. * Learn how to ask someone their name. * Practice greeting and introducing others in a friendly way. **2. Main Lesson Content:** **(Display bright, colorful images of children greeting each other)** **Hello & Goodbye:** When we meet someone new, we say "Hello!" or "Hi!". It's like saying, "It's nice to see you!". When we leave, we say "Goodbye!" or "Bye!". It means "See you later!". **(Show a simple animation of a child waving as they say goodbye.)** **Introducing Yourself:** We introduce ourselves by saying our name. For example: "Hello, my name is [Teacher's Name/Character's Name]". You can also say "Hi, I'm [Your Name]". **(Show an animation of a child pointing to themselves and saying their name.)** **Asking Someone's Name:** If you want to know someone's name, you can ask: "What's your name?" or "What is your name?". Remember to listen carefully when they tell you! **(Show an animation of one child asking another child their name.)** **Putting it all together:** We can combine these! For example: * "Hello! My name is Lily. What's your name?" * "Hi, I'm Tom. It's nice to meet you, Sarah!" **3. Interactive Activity: "Name Train"** **(Use a gamified element. For a learning app, this could be animated characters.)** Children take turns being the "engine" of a name train. The engine says their name and then says "choo choo!". Each following child adds their name to the train, repeating the previous names and adding their own. For example: * Child 1: "My name is Mia! Choochoo!" * Child 2: "My name is Mia, and my name is Ben! Choochoo!" * Child 3: "My name is Mia, my name is Ben, and my name is Alex! Choochoo!" **4. Practice Exercises:** **(Display images representing different scenarios.)** **Scenario 1:** You see your friend at the park. What do you say? (Answer: Hello!/Hi! [Friend's Name]!) **Scenario 2:** A new child joins your class. How do you introduce yourself? (Answer: Hello, my name is [Your Name].) **Scenario 3:** You want to know the name of the new child. What do you ask? (Answer: What's your name? / What is your name?) **Scenario 4:** It's time to go home. What do you say to your teacher? (Answer: Goodbye!/Bye!) **5. Assessment Questions:** **(Multiple-choice questions with appealing visuals.)** 1. What do you say when you meet someone new? a) Goodbye! b) Hello! c) Thank you! 2. How do you introduce yourself? a) "What's your name?" b) "My name is [Your Name]." c) "Goodbye!" 3. What do you say to ask someone their name? a) "Hello!" b) "What's your name?" c) "Bye!" **(For a learning app, provide immediate feedback on the answers. Consider using positive reinforcement like cheerful sounds or animations for correct answers.)** This lesson plan can be easily adapted for use in a learning app by incorporating interactive elements, animations, and gamification to make learning fun and engaging for young children.
