Content Requirements:

For each subject, create separate sections for 'Year 1 (Ages 5-6)' and 'Year 2 (Ages 6-7)', with a brief mention of 'Early Year 3 (Ages 7-8)' concepts where appropriate.
Language Style: Use very simple, child-friendly language. Avoid jargon.
Explanations: Concise and easy to understand.
Visuals: Suggest accompanying visual elements (e.g., "Image: Colourful diagram of a plant").
Interactive Elements: Suggest types of interactive activities (e.g., "Activity: Drag-and-drop game").
Examples: Provide clear, simple examples relevant to the age group.
Practice: Include simple practice questions or activity ideas.
Tone: Engaging, encouraging, and positive.
Detailed Content for Brain Buddy App Folders (Ages 6-8):
1. Maths Folder
Year 1 (Ages 5-6):
Topic: Numbers to 20
Explanation: "Let's learn about numbers! We can count how many things there are, like toys or friends. Numbers help us know 'how many'."
Activity: "Match the number to the correct group of objects (e.g., connect '3' to three apples)."
Example: "Count the stars: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. There are 5 stars!" (Image: 5 stars)
Practice: "How many balloons? (Image: 7 balloons)"
Topic: Adding & Subtracting to 10
Explanation: "Adding means putting things together to find the total. Subtracting means taking things away to find what's left."
Activity: "Interactive: Click on fingers to show addition (e.g., 3 + 2 = ?)."
Example: "3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples. (Image: 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples)"
Practice: "5 birds - 2 birds = ? birds (Image: 5 birds, 2 flying away)"
Topic: Shapes & Patterns
Explanation: "Shapes are all around us! Circles are round, squares have four equal sides."
Visual: "Image: Pictures of a circle, square, triangle, rectangle."
Activity: "Find the circles in the picture of a house."
Practice: "What shape is this? (Image: a triangle)"
Topic: Time (O'clock & Half Past)
Explanation: "Clocks help us know when to play or eat! O'clock is when the big hand points to 12. Half past is when it points to 6."
Visual: "Image: Clock faces showing '3 o'clock' and 'half past 7'."
Activity: "Match the clock to the time word (e.g., connect clock showing '9:00' to 'Nine o'clock')."
Year 2 (Ages 6-7):
Topic: Numbers to 100 & Place Value
Explanation: "Numbers can be big! 'Place value' tells us what a digit is worth. In 23, the '2' means 2 tens, and the '3' means 3 ones."
Visual: "Image: Number 47 with '4 tens' and '7 ones' highlighted."
Activity: "Build a number: Drag blocks (tens and ones) to make 54."
Practice: "What does the '6' mean in 61? (6 ones / 6 tens)"
Topic: Multiplication & Division (2s, 5s, 10s)
Explanation: "Multiplication is like adding groups quickly (3 groups of 2). Division is sharing things equally."
Activity: "Times table pop-quiz game (e.g., 2 x 4 = ?)."
Example: "2 groups of 5 sweets is 10 sweets (2 x 5 = 10). (Image: 2 bags, 5 sweets in each)"
Practice: "Share 10 cookies between 2 friends. How many do they get each?"
Topic: Fractions (Halves, Quarters, Thirds)
Explanation: "Fractions are parts of a whole! Half means 1 out of 2 equal parts. Quarter means 1 out of 4 equal parts."
Visual: "Image: Pizza cut into halves, quarters, thirds."
Activity: "Drag and drop the correct fraction (e.g., place '1/2' on a half-shaded circle)."
Practice: "Colour in one quarter of the shape. (Image: a circle divided into 4 quarters)"
Topic: Money & Change
Explanation: "We use money to buy things! Let's learn to count coins and work out change."
Visual: "Image: UK coins (1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1, £2)."
Activity: "Shopping game: Pick coins to buy an item (e.g., a toy car for 35p)."
Practice: "If you buy a sweet for 10p and pay with 20p, how much change do you get?"
Early Year 3 (Ages 7-8):
Topic: Numbers to 1000
Explanation: "Numbers can go up to hundreds! In 345, '3' means 3 hundreds, '4' means 4 tens, and '5' means 5 ones."
Visual: "Image: Place value chart for hundreds, tens, ones."
Practice: "What number is 7 hundreds, 2 tens, and 8 ones?"
