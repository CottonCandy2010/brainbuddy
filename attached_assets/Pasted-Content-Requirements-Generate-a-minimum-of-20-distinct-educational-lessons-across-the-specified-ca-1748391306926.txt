Content Requirements:

Generate a minimum of 20 distinct educational lessons across the specified categories.
For each lesson:
Provide a clear, child-friendly 'Lesson Title'.
A concise 'Explanation' of the concept.
A specific 'Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion'.

🔢 Counting and Numbers (Easy)
Lesson 1: Counting to 10

Explanation: "Let's count! Numbers tell us how many things we have. We'll start by counting from 1 to 10."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Bubble Pop Count: Pop bubbles with numbers 1-10 in order."
Example: "Count the balloons: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6. There are 6 balloons!"
Practice Question: "How many ducks are in the pond? (Image: 8 ducks)"
Visual Aid: Image of a number line 1-10 with corresponding quantities of objects.
Lesson 2: Number Recognition to 20

Explanation: "Can you spot the numbers? We'll learn what numbers 1 to 20 look like and what they mean."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Number Match: Match the numeral (e.g., '12') to the word ('twelve')."
Example: "This is the number 15. (Image: Large numeral '15')"
Practice Question: "Which number is this? (Image: Large numeral '7')"
Visual Aid: Flashcards showing numerals 1-20.
Lesson 3: Counting in 1s, 2s, 5s, and 10s

Explanation: "Counting can be super speedy! We can count in steps, like counting by 2s or 10s."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Skip Count Hop: Hop on a number line, counting in 2s (2, 4, 6...). "
Example: "Counting in 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20."
Practice Question: "What comes next when counting in 10s? 10, 20, 30, ?"
Visual Aid: Animated characters skipping on a number line.
Lesson 4: Before, After, and Between (Numbers to 20)

Explanation: "Numbers have neighbours! Some numbers come before, some come after, and some are in between."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Missing Number Bridge: Fill in the number that comes before, after, or between."
Example: "What comes after 8? (Answer: 9)"
Practice Question: "What number is between 12 and 14?"
Visual Aid: Three houses with numbers, showing which one is "before," "after," or "in between."
Lesson 5: Place Value (Tens and Ones to 20)

Explanation: "Big numbers are made of smaller groups! 'Place value' tells us how many groups of 10 and how many single ones a number has."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Build a Number: Drag 'ten' sticks and 'one' blocks to make a number (e.g., for 14, drag one 'ten' stick and four 'one' blocks)."
Example: "The number 17 is 1 ten and 7 ones. (Image: 1 bundle of 10 sticks and 7 loose sticks)"
Practice Question: "How many tens and ones are in 13?"
Visual Aid: Image of a tens frame filled with 10 dots, and loose dots for ones.
➕ Adding and Subtracting (Easy)
Lesson 6: Adding with Objects (to 10)

Explanation: "Adding means putting groups together to find the total! Let's use our toys to help us."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Toy Collector: Drag 3 red cars and 2 blue cars into a box. How many now?"
Example: "4 stars + 3 stars = 7 stars."
Practice Question: "You have 5 apples. Your friend gives you 3 more. How many do you have now?"
Visual Aid: Simple cartoon drawing of objects being combined.
Lesson 7: Subtracting by Taking Away (to 10)

Explanation: "Subtracting means taking some away! How many are left?"
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Cookie Muncher: Start with 7 cookies. Click to 'eat' 3. How many are left?"
Example: "8 birds - 2 birds = 6 birds."
Practice Question: "There are 9 flowers. You pick 4. How many are left?"
Visual Aid: Animated scene of objects disappearing from a group.
Lesson 8: Number Bonds to 10

Explanation: "Number bonds are pairs of numbers that add up to a special total, like 10! Knowing these helps us add fast."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Number Bond Pairs: Match the numbers that make 10 (e.g., 3 to 7)."
Example: "7 + 3 = 10."
Practice Question: "What number goes with 4 to make 10?"
Visual Aid: Ten frames with dots filling up.
Lesson 9: Addition on a Number Line

Explanation: "A number line helps us jump to find the answer! To add, we jump forwards."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Froggy Jumps: Drag a frog on a number line to show 5 + 3."
Example: "To do 6 + 2, start at 6 and jump 2 times forwards. You land on 8."
Practice Question: "Use the number line to find 7 + 3." (Image: Number line 0-10)
Visual Aid: Animated jumps on a number line.
Lesson 10: Subtraction on a Number Line

Explanation: "To subtract on a number line, we jump backwards! How many jumps back did we make?"
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Rabbit Hops Back: Drag a rabbit on a number line to show 9 - 4."
Example: "To do 10 - 3, start at 10 and jump 3 times backwards. You land on 7."
Practice Question: "Use the number line to find 8 - 5." (Image: Number line 0-10)
Visual Aid: Animated backward jumps on a number line.
🔷 Shapes and Patterns (Easy)
Lesson 11: Recognising 2D Shapes

Explanation: "Shapes are all around us! 2D shapes are flat, like a picture. Let's find circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles!"
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Shape Sorter: Drag shapes into the correct bin (e.g., 'Square Bin')."
Example: "A door is often a rectangle. (Image: Door)"
Practice Question: "Which shape has three sides? (Image: A triangle, a circle, a square)"
Visual Aid: Colourful cartoon representations of 2D shapes.
Lesson 12: Recognising 3D Shapes

Explanation: "3D shapes are 'fat' shapes, not flat! You can hold them. Like a ball is a sphere, and a box is a cube."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Spin and Name: Spin a 3D shape and guess its name."
Example: "A dice is a cube. (Image: Dice)"
Practice Question: "What 3D shape is a can of soup?"
Visual Aid: Rotatable 3D models of cubes, spheres, cylinders, cones.
Lesson 13: Making Simple Patterns

Explanation: "Patterns are things that repeat! Like red, blue, red, blue. Can you spot the rule?"
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Pattern Builder: Drag and drop coloured blocks to complete an ABAB or ABCABC pattern."
Example: "Circle, Square, Circle, Square, Circle, ____ (Answer: Square)"
Practice Question: "What comes next in this pattern? Apple, Banana, Apple, Banana, Apple, ____"
Visual Aid: Animated repeating patterns.
Lesson 14: Identifying Properties of 2D Shapes

Explanation: "Shapes have special parts! 'Sides' are the straight lines, and 'vertices' (corners) are the pointy bits where sides meet."
Interactive Activity / Game Suggestion: "Shape Explorer: Click on a shape, and it highlights its sides and vertices."
Example: "A triangle has 3 sides and 3 vertices."
Practice Question: "How many sides does a square have?"
Visual Aid: Animated highlights of sides and vertices on different 2D shapes.
